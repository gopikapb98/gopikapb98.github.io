About Me

I'm a computational and applied mathematician pursuing doctoral research under Dr. Nagaiah Chamakuri, driven by a passion for developing innovative algorithms and computational methods to tackle complex real-world challenges in mathematical biology.

My work pioneers efficient and robust numerical schemes to model the electrophysiology, mechanics, and electromechanics of the heart, ensuring high biophysical accuracy across multiple spatial and temporal scales. Leveraging high-performance parallel computing, I strive to bridge the computational challenges inherent in cardiac electromechanical modeling. Committed to interdisciplinary collaboration and STEM advocacy, I'm devoted to advancing the frontiers of mathematical science and its real-world applications.
